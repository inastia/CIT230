The Blizzard's Bills Come Due

The storm that paralyzed wide swaths of the state early last week also set off a new blizzard of worries virtually everywhere about state and local government finances. And even more budget-busting problems threatened as warmer weather brought the possibility of flooding this weekend. Mayor Sharpe James said that the blizzard might cost the city more than $1 million. "My budget will be depleted because of this storm," said Mr. James, who had 80 workers reporting for 12-hour shifts once the snow started last Sunday. "My salt and sand allotments are gone."
